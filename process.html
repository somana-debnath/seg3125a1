My Process.

How I approach design problems, and where that approach comes from.

Background

How I got here.

My experience with web development started in grade 10, when I built my first webpage out of curiosity. At the time, I had no formal instruction and relied entirely on YouTube tutorials and trial and error. That process of searching, experimenting, and figuring things out independently is largely how I still learn today.

Since then I have built pages and interfaces as part of personal projects, academic coursework, and co-op terms. I use Figma for wireframing and prototyping before moving into code, which helps me separate design decisions from implementation details.

Approach

How I work through a project.

1

Define the purpose

Before making any design decisions, I focus on what the product needs to accomplish and who will be using it. A clear understanding of the goal informs every choice that follows.

2

Structure the content

I organize information before thinking about visual presentation. This means deciding what content exists, how it relates, and what order makes the most sense to a user moving through the interface.

3

Apply visual principles

Once the structure is solid, I focus on visual communication: hierarchy, contrast, spacing, and consistency. Every design choice should serve the content, not the other way around.

4

Build and test in code

I prototype directly in HTML and CSS rather than static mockups. Working in the browser means I can evaluate real interactions, responsiveness, and behaviour rather than approximations of them.
